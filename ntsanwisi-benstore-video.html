Gazankulu

The film explores the history and development of Gazankulu, a bantustan established by the apartheid government as a semi-autonomous homeland for the Tsonga people. Created in the 1960s in the northern region of the former Transvaal province, Gazankulu was granted self-governing status in 1973

Through archival footage, interviews, and historical analysis, the film highlights the social, cultural, and economic changes within Gazankulu, showing how the community navigated the restrictions of apartheid while striving for local governance, cultural preservation, and development. It provides insight into both the challenges imposed by the apartheid system and the efforts of the Tsonga people to assert their identity and manage their homeland.
