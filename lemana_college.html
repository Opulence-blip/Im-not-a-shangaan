Lemana Renovation in 2013

The renovation of Lemana College, situated near Elim in Limpopo's Vhembe district, into a secondary school has been plagued by disputes and ongoing interruptions, with expenditure escalating to multi-million rand levels.

Initiated in 2013 with an original completion timeline of a year and a half(18 months), the project remains unfinished after almost a decade. By the close of the previous year, students were relocated back to the original school building several kilometres away because of the new site's substandard infrastructural state.



This is the state of Lemana College Below

The protracted renovation of Lemana College in Limpopo's Vhembe district continues to face significant setbacks. According to a February 2020 GroundUp report, the project's first contractor entered liquidation, leading to the contract's termination in 2015. A second contractor commenced work in 2018 but was forced to stop due to labour disputes.

There is now growing concern from the Waterval Community Development Forum and the Lemana Task Team that further delays are imminent. Representatives from these groups report that severe vandalism at the construction site is likely to cause additional holdups and incur extra costs, pushing the project's budget even higher.

Lemana Task Team highlighted that the first phase was intended to complete all classrooms and the administration block. Makondo expressed the community's frustration, stating, “We feel it’s unacceptable for the department of education to take more than eight years to do renovations. This is so disturbing.”
